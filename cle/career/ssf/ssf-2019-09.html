Sea Shore Flow

September 2019 update

Sea/Shore Flow (SSF) policy determines how long a Sailor spends on a given tour of duty throughout a career. The timing is based on the demand for Sailors of a given rating at sea and at shore. Ratings with many sea duty billets and only few shore duty billets will tend to have longer sea tours and shorter shore tours, and vice versa, although Navy policy sets limits on the amount of time any Sailor may spend on a single sea tour.

Nuclear Ratings

The nuclear-trained ratings are not controlled by Sea/Shore policy. Instead, refer to reference (c) and reference (d) for information on tour length.

This applies to these ratings:

  • EMN(SS)
  • EMN(SW)
  • ETN(SS)
  • ETN(SW)
  • MMN(SS)
  • MMN(SW)
  • MMNELT(SS)
  • MMNELT(SW)

Unusual career paths

Some ratings are not defined by Sea/Shore Flow by the nature of their duties. This applies to these ratings:

  • AC
  • CMD
  • CTI
  • CTN
  • HM
  • LCAC
  • MU
  • NCCR
